Who I am

I believe, at the deepest level, that everything comes down to fulfillment. I have slowly been pushing and pushing myself till finally, I feel that I am reaping the rewards in terms of capability. I learned computer science at a very fundamental level, all the way up to a higher level. Now, I feel that I have a lot of value to offer and a unique perspective on things because I have not gone the traditional path. I have traveled a lot and learned who I am. I am someone who enjoys the process of learning and absorbing as much as possible. I have found that the whole point of life is to follow one's curiosities. Eventually, this will lead to what we are best at, and what is most fulfilling to us.

As a child, I was most curious about how everything worked. I wanted to know what was inside electronics, so I would smash them on the ground. I was fascinated by gutting fish because I got to see how they worked. I slowly started building up this library inside my own head, until I started designing/building my own gadgets. This was only a hobby, yet I knew even then that I wanted to do this for the rest of my life. Reading about the great innovators of the eras, I wanted to do what they did; build solutions to problems and help create the future.

After learning my skill-set, coding, I felt that I had more to contribute than just code, because of my intangible skills. I enjoy working with people to accomplish a goal and enjoy helping people work through a problem. After joining as a high school software/robotics mentor, I have found myself becoming a more confident and competent leader. I am developing these abilities and really enjoy the process. I am someone who will continue to push myself past my comfort zone because I want to be more comfortable in stressful situations. That feeling when my legs are shaking from nerves actually excites me, because they will shake a bit less next time.
